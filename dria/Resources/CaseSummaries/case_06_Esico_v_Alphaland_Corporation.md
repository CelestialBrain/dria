# Esico v. Alphaland Corporation

Summary: Jose Esico, a pilot, received specialized training from Alphaland, agreeing to serve for five years or reimburse the pro-rated cost. He resigned after two years, claiming constructive dismissal, and Alphaland demanded the training reimbursement. The Supreme Court ruled that Alphaland's claim for reimbursement was a civil law matter, not a labor dispute, so the labor arbiter had no jurisdiction over it.

LL: Don't file civil law claims, like breaches of training bond contracts, in labor tribunals; they belong in regular courts. Always remember that a training bond with a service requirement creates reciprocal obligations, and its breach falls under Article 1191 of the Civil Code, allowing for specific performance or damages.
