# Bongcayao v. CONFED

Summary: Bongcayao's company, VHB Biopro, promised to deliver fertilizers to CONFED, which opened a huge Letter of Credit as agreed. But Bongcayao failed to deliver and tried to nullify the contract. CONFED sued for P30M in lost profits, but the Supreme Court found that amount too speculative. Instead, the SC made Bongcayao pay P4M in temperate damages because CONFED clearly suffered a loss, even if the exact amount couldn't be proven.

LL: Always make sure to have solid, quantifiable proof for actual damages like lost profits; don't just estimate. If you can't prove the exact amount, the court might award temperate damages under Article 2224 of the Civil Code if a pecuniary loss is certain. Remember that simple breach of contract without bad faith usually doesn't warrant moral damages.
