# Tanke v. Development Bank of the Philippines, et al.

Summary: Alejandro Tankeh, a doctor and businessman, signed a solidary loan agreement with DBP for his brother Ruperto's shipping company, SSLI, after Ruperto promised him shares and a board seat. When SSLI defaulted, Alejandro claimed he was defrauded by Ruperto. The Supreme Court ruled that while Ruperto committed incidental fraud (*dolo incidente*) by not fulfilling his promises, Alejandro's sophistication meant it wasn't causal fraud (*dolo causante*) that would void the contract. Thus, Alejandro remained solidarily liable to DBP/APT but could claim damages from Ruperto.
