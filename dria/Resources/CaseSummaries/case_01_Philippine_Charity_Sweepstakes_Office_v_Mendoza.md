# Philippine Charity Sweepstakes Office v. Mendoza

Summary: Antonio Mendoza won the lottery, but his granddaughter crumpled his ticket, and his daughter accidentally ironed it black. The PCSO refused to pay his P12 million prize, citing their "no ticket, no payment" rule. The Supreme Court sided with Mendoza, ordering PCSO to pay because his winning numbers were clearly recorded in their system, and the damaged ticket was just physical proof, not the actual win.

LL: Always remember that in contracts of adhesion, like lottery tickets, any ambiguity in the rules is interpreted against the party who drafted them (like PCSO), not the consumer (Art. 1377). The court will look at the true intention and substance of the agreement, not just strict literal interpretations of physical evidence.
